    The New Riddle of Induction

    Have rules for drawing inferences, and change those over time.

    The new riddle is to explain how we actually use induction. In which ways will the future resember the past?

    The Grue Paradox

    Definition. A thing is grue if and only if it is first observed before time \(t\) to be green or after time \(t\) as blue.

    Definition. A thing is bleen if and only if it is first observed before time \(t\) to be blue or after time \(t\) as green.

    These predicates are weird and aren't useful for accurate predictions. But what's so strange about them? Can define "green" and "blue" in terms of them, but "green" and "blue" aren't treated strangely.

    Note. We have to decide what we want to count as a change and not a change.

    Looking forward. In looking at the concept of entrenchment, Goodman resolves the problem of grue by declaring is not-enchrenched. This is in fact true, but is not really a satisfying answer to our inquiry into the problem with grue. Epistemically, what's the problem with grue? He just explains how we do not use grue.
